# **Traffic & Accessibility Potential Rubric (Location-Oriented)**

## **Evaluation Criteria**

Score the *location’s accessibility* on a scale of **0–10**, based on transit access, walkability, and traffic flow.

---

## **1. Public Transit & Connectivity Quality (35%)**

### Excellent (9–10)

* Multiple transit lines or modes (bus, metro, subway)
* Short walking distance to stops/stations
* High service frequency and convenience

### Good (7–8)

* Strong transit access with reasonable walking proximity

### Adequate (5–6)

* Basic transit coverage; some accessibility gaps

### Poor (3–4)

* Sparse transit infrastructure

### Insufficient (0–2)

* Minimal or no transit access

---

## **2. Walkability, Parking & Road Access (25%)**

### Excellent

* Strong walkability, safe sidewalks
* Sufficient parking availability
* Clear road network and easy vehicle access

### Good

* Generally walkable; some parking; workable road access

### Adequate

* Mixed accessibility conditions

### Poor

* Difficult pedestrian or vehicle access

### Insufficient

* Access barriers severely limit store viability

---

## **3. Target Customer Mobility Fit (20%)**

### Excellent

* Accessibility modes align perfectly with target customer behavior (e.g., students + subway; families + parking)

### Good

* Mostly aligned

### Adequate

* Some alignment; partial mismatch

### Poor

* Little alignment with customer mobility patterns

### Insufficient

* Accessibility poorly suited for target customers

---

## **4. Traffic Volume & Temporal Benefits (20%)**

### Excellent

* Strong and predictable peak traffic (commuters, school, weekend shoppers)
* High visibility and natural customer flow

### Good

* Solid traffic volumes; clear peak periods

### Adequate

* Moderate but usable traffic flow

### Poor

* Limited foot or vehicle traffic

### Insufficient

* Very low traffic; poor business visibility

---

## **Output Format**

```json
{
  "score": 7.9,
  "justification": "The site has strong subway and bus coverage, excellent walkability, and traffic peaks aligned with morning commuters. Despite limited parking, accessibility suits the transit-heavy customer demographic well."
}
```
