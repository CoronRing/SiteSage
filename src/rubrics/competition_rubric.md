## **1. Competitor Density & Proximity (35%)**

### Excellent (9–10)

* Balanced competition levels
* Nearby competitors validate demand but do not overcrowd the market
* No direct competitor extremely close

### Good (7–8)

* Manageable density; not oversaturated

### Adequate (5–6)

* Several competitors but still viable

### Poor (3–4)

* High density; competition may limit viability

### Insufficient (0–2)

* Severe clustering; little chance to compete

---

## **2. Market Saturation vs. Demand (30%)**

### Excellent

* Strong unmet demand
* High customer-to-store ratio favorable for new entry

### Good

* Demand likely supports additional competitors

### Adequate

* Balanced but uncertain capacity for new store

### Poor

* Demand appears insufficient for new entry

### Insufficient

* Market fully saturated or shrinking

---

## **3. Positioning Opportunity (20%)**

### Excellent

* Clear niche or unmet need (e.g., premium, budget, unique concept)
* Strong differentiators available

### Good

* Some positioning options

### Adequate

* Limited differentiation space

### Poor

* Hard to differentiate meaningfully

### Insufficient

* No viable positioning strategy apparent

---

## **4. Competitive Risk (15%)**

### Excellent

* Low competitive risk; complementary neighbors

### Good

* Moderate, manageable risk

### Adequate

* Some risk from established competitors

### Poor

* High risk; strong incumbents nearby

### Insufficient

* Very high risk environment

---
