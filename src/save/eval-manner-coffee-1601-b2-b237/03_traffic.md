- For coffee customers:
  - A realistic “park → coffee → main activity” pattern: **3–7 minutes** walk from car to B2 store, depending on level and elevator/escalator connections.

### 4.2 Detour vs. natural paths

Assume average walking speed **4.5–5.0 km/h** (~75–83 m/min).

#### From Jing’an Temple Metro Station → Offices / Malls

- Natural path for many commuters:
  - Exit at station → walk **directly along Nanjing West Road** to office (Plaza 66, Kerry, etc.).  
  - Typical direct distance: **150–400 m** → **2–6 minutes**.
- To visit this store:
  - Exit → enter Réel Mall (often via internal metro–mall connection) → go down to B2 → walk along F&B corridor to B237.  
  - Additional vertical travel + corridor walking: **100–250 m equivalent**, plus 1–2 escalator rides.
- Extra time vs shortest path along Nanjing West Road:
  - **~2–4 minutes one-way** for most commuters whose office doesn’t require passing through B2.  
  - Round-trip detour for a coffee-only stop: **~4–8 minutes** over their no-stop route.

#### From nearby Nanjing West Road offices (north side, 0–400 m)

- Direct path office → nearest street café/mall entrance: **50–250 m** → **1–4 minutes**.  
- To reach B2–B237:
  - Office → mall entrance: 1–4 minutes.  
  - Down to B2 + corridor: +**1–3 minutes**.
- Additional detour relative to using:
  - Ground floor or street-front coffee: **+1–3 minutes** each way.  
- Net extra detour: typically **2–5 minutes round-trip**.

#### From residents within 800 m

- 800 m walk = **~10–11 minutes**.  
- B2 depth adds:
  - **1–3 extra minutes** compared to a ground-floor or street-side café.  
- Relative decrease in convenience: **~10–25% more time** vs at-grade destination cafés on same street.

### 4.3 5-min vs 10-min detour catchment

Focus on people already moving through Jing’an Temple / Nanjing West Road area, not total population.

A 5-minute detour ≈ **375–415 m** extra walking (round trip) or **190–210 m one-way**.

Group estimates who can reasonably add ≤5 minutes to existing routes:

- **Office workers (50k–70k within 800 m)**  
  - Those whose commute/lunch paths already pass through/near Réel: assume **20–30%**.  
  - Within 5-min detour tolerance: **~10,000–21,000 workers**.

To avoid double-counting overlap, a reasonable compressed figure:

- **People effectively within a ≤5-minute detour radius to this store during their normal activity:**  
  - **~30,000–50,000 individuals/day** (including mall shoppers, office workers, commuters, tourists).

#### 10-minute detour range

A 10-minute detour ≈ **750–830 m** extra walking (round trip).

Additional capture beyond the 5-min set:

Effective daily *reachable* population (within ≤10-min detour of existing routes) is thus on the order of:

- **~50,000–80,000 unique people/day** moving within paths that can reasonably route through the shop’s location, matching earlier passer-by estimates (15–30k in front of store after significant filtering).

## 5. Visibility analysis

### External visibility (street/road)

- The store is on **B2**, inside Réel Mall:
  - **No direct street frontage** on Nanjing West Road or Yan’an Road.  
  - No visibility to **car passengers** on Yan’an Elevated Road.  
- From the map + local structure:
  - The large **temple + park block** sits between main traffic flows and the internal B2 corridor.  
  - People walking along Nanjing West Road mostly see:
    - Temple façade.  
    - Street-front malls and facades at or near Jing’an Temple intersection.  
  - Without **strong external signage** or campaign, walk-in discovery from casual street pedestrians is limited.

### Internal mall visibility

- B2 level is a **core F&B layer**:
  - There are many F&B units (multiple coffee, tea, dessert, and casual dining brands within **0–50 m** of the store location, as shown by the dense restaurant list).  
  - Immediate competitors (M Stand, other coffee and beverage brands) are within **10–50 m** of the store.
- For customers already on B2:
  - Store visibility is **strongly dependent on corridor placement**, sightlines, and signage.  
  - Passer-by volume directly in front of the shop: **~15,000–30,000 people/day**.  
  - Competing banners and shopfronts will visually “crowd” the environment—brand recognition and façade design becomes critical.

**Net effect**

- **Area-level visibility (district scale):** mostly via mall brand + digital search, not from the road.  
- **Micro-level visibility (inside B2):** high, but in a competitive cluster; customers can see many alternatives within seconds.

## 6. Summary: Pros and Cons of the location (traffic perspective)

### Pros

- **Exceptional public transit access**
  - Jing’an Temple station within **~60–200 m** of mall entrances, 3 metro lines, **250–300k** riders/day.  
  - Multiple bus/BRT stops within **200–350 m**.

- **Large, dense, high-income catchment**
  - **~39k residents within 800 m** and **~184k within 1.5 km**, with majority working-age.  
  - **~50–70k office workers** and **~1.4–2.1k hotel guests/night** nearby.  
  - Natural daily pedestrian flow in front of shop: **~15–30k people/day** on B2.

- **Weather-proof and time-extended traffic**
  - Indoor, climate-controlled environment; mall traffic remains in **hot, cold, and rainy** conditions.  
  - B2 F&B keeps reasonable traffic beyond office hours (evenings, weekends).

- **Short detour for many core users**
  - For people already in Réel or transiting between metro and nearby offices, incremental detour to the shop is:
    - **~2–4 extra minutes** from metro routes.  
    - **~2–5 extra minutes round-trip** compared with using a streetfront alternative.

- **Large 5–10 minute detour catchment**
  - Effective **≤5-min detour** reachable population: **~30–50k people/day**.  
  - **≤10-min detour**: **~50–80k people/day**.

### Cons

- **No street or vehicular visibility**
  - Located on **B2 without frontage**; no direct exposure to Nanjing West Road or Yan’an Road traffic.  
  - Discovery heavily reliant on:
    - Mall wayfinding.  
    - Digital maps and delivery apps.  
    - Word-of-mouth and targeted campaigns.

- **Additional vertical detour**
  - Compared with ground-floor or street cafés:
    - Extra **1–3 minutes each way** for escalators/elevators and corridor walk.  
  - This slightly favors competitors at **metro exits or street level** when customers are in a hurry.

- **High competitive density in immediate corridor**
  - Multiple coffee/tea/dessert brands within **10–50 m**:
    - Quick substitution if queues or pricing are not competitive.  
  - Requires **clear differentiation** (product, speed, seating, or brand positioning) to capture meaningful share of the **15–30k daily corridor passers-by**.

- **Block configuration limits spontaneous footfall from some directions**
  - Yan’an Elevated Road to the south and temple/park block layout reduce **direct north–south pedestrian permeability**.  
  - Some potential users (especially those south of Yan’an Road) face less convenient crossings and may prefer options nearer to their side of the elevated road.

---

In conclusion, from a traffic standpoint, this location benefits from **extremely strong transit-fed and mall-internal footfall** and a **large reachable population within modest detour times**, but sacrifices **street visibility** and must compete intensely for attention on a crowded B2 F&B corridor.
