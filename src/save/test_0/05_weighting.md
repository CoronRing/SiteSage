# Weighting Rationale

## Overview

**Store type:** Coffee Shop / Café  
**Location context:** Inside Kerry Centre Mall (unit E1-03) on Nanjing West Road  
**Service mode:** Dine-in and takeaway  
**Target customers:** Office workers, shoppers, mall visitors, commuters  
**Price level:** Mid-range  
**Time window:** Daytime and evening

This concept is a high-frequency, convenience-oriented coffee shop embedded in a premium mixed-use retail and office environment. The business model relies on regular daily visits (morning, lunchtime, afternoon) and opportunistic purchases from existing mall and street traffic.

According to the rubric, coffee shops generally receive:
- Customer: 0.30–0.33
- Traffic & Accessibility: 0.35–0.40
- Competition: 0.30–0.35

The weights below are tailored to this specific mall-based, commuter- and office-worker-heavy context.

## Final Weights

- **Customer:** 0.31  
- **Traffic & Accessibility:** 0.39  
- **Competition:** 0.30  

_Total: 1.00_

---

## 1. Customer Weight (0.31)

### Role of Customer Profile

The shop targets a relatively broad but still somewhat defined segment:
- **Office workers** in and around Kerry Centre towers
- **Shoppers and mall visitors** visiting retail and dining
- **Commuters** passing through the area

Key aspects:
- **Income and willingness to pay:** Mid-range pricing requires a base of customers comfortable with paying a premium over convenience stores or low-end beverage outlets.
- **Lifestyle and habits:** Office workers and commuters are heavy users of daily coffee and tea beverages, and may develop habitual purchase patterns.
- **Daypart behavior:** Different segments dominate different times (office workers in mornings and lunch; shoppers in afternoons and weekends).

### Why Not Higher or Lower?

- Not **high** (0.35–0.40) because this is **not a niche concept**; it serves a mainstream urban-professional and shopper base rather than a narrow subculture or membership model.
- Not **very low** because **demographic and psychographic fit still matters**—if the surrounding office and mall population had very low disposable income or limited coffee consumption habits, performance would suffer even with good traffic.

Thus, a **moderate weight of 0.31** reflects that the concept needs a compatible customer base but does not hinge on ultra-precise demographic targeting.

---

## 2. Traffic & Accessibility Weight (0.39)

### Centrality of Traffic for This Concept

For this coffee shop, **traffic is the primary success driver**.

- **Mall internal traffic:** Being inside Kerry Centre Mall, the store’s performance is highly sensitive to:
  - Proximity to main entrances
  - Visibility from primary corridors and atrium spaces
  - Location near office tower lobbies, escalators, elevators, or transit links
- **Routine and impulse visits:**
  - Office workers may buy coffee daily on their way in, during breaks, and before meetings.
  - Shoppers and general mall visitors make opportunistic purchases when passing by.
- **Service mode & time window:** Dine-in and takeaway over daytime and evening amplify the importance of easy access from workspaces and retail zones.

The business is not a destination fine-dining concept; **customers generally will not cross the city just to visit this location**. Instead, they choose it because it is convenient given where they already are.

### Why Near the Top of the Range?

- The rubric suggests **0.35–0.40** for coffee shops, especially when commuter and foot traffic are critical.
- A mall-based location with dense office and retail activity fits exactly this profile.

Therefore, **0.39** is appropriate: near the top of the recommended range to reflect the dominant role of internal mall and commuter flows while keeping some space for customer and competition factors.

---

## 3. Competition Weight (0.30)

### Nature of Competition

In a high-profile urban mall:
- There are likely **multiple direct and indirect competitors**:
  - Other coffee chains or independent cafés
  - Bakeries, tea shops, juice bars, and dessert outlets that can substitute for coffee occasions
- Customer switching costs are **low**: if quality and convenience are similar, minor differences in distance, wait time, or promotions can drive choice.

### Why Moderate but Not Dominant?

However, several dynamics temper the weight of competition:
- **Micro-location often trumps brand differences** inside malls. A coffee shop positioned closer to office tower exits or main escalators can outperform better-known brands that are less conveniently placed.
- The concept is **mid-range** and can differentiate through:
  - Menu variety and beverage quality
  - Speed of service (important for office workers on breaks)
  - Ambiance and seating comfort for short meetings or laptop work

As a result:
- Competition is **important** but does not overshadow traffic/accessibility.
- It fits best at the **low end of the moderate range** for coffee shops.

Thus, **0.30** is chosen, acknowledging competitive pressure without overstating it relative to accessibility.
