## Domain-by-Domain Justification

### 1. Customer (0.31)

**Role for this concept:** Important but not dominant.

- The café is in a **mixed but lifestyle-oriented catchment**: residents, office workers, and tourists. This is not a hyper-niche concept limited to a very narrow demographic; it serves a relatively broad urban audience that already frequents Wukang Road.
- However, the area tends to attract **higher-spending, experience-seeking customers** (young professionals, students, tourists, and middle-to-high income locals). Matching their expectations on price, ambiance, and product quality is important.
- Coffee consumption is **habitual and repeat-based**; building a base of regulars from nearby offices and residences is key. This supports a customer weight slightly above the minimum for broad-appeal concepts, but still within the coffee-shop band.

**Why 0.31 (not higher or lower):**
- Higher (0.33–0.35) would be more appropriate for a niche or strongly segmented concept (e.g., specialty third-wave shop targeting only coffee enthusiasts or a members-only workspace café). We lack evidence of such a narrow focus.
- Lower (≤0.30) would fit a pure convenience or commodity model; here, the experiential and lifestyle aspects of the area mean customer fit matters more than in a gas-station or pure convenience context.

### 2. Traffic & Accessibility (0.39)

**Role for this concept:** Primary driver of success.

- Coffee shops rely on **high visit frequency** and a mix of planned and impulse visits. In a destination corridor like Wukang Road, **foot traffic volume and flow patterns** are critical.
- 武康庭 is a **multi-tenant complex**; internal positioning (visibility from main entrances, proximity to anchor tenants, signage, ease of wayfinding) will strongly influence capture of both internal and street traffic.
- Many customers will be **walk-in visitors**: tourists exploring the street, shoppers in 武康庭, and locals strolling the neighborhood. For these, convenience (seeing the shop, easy entry, minimal detour) is decisive.
- Public transit and walkability in Xuhui are generally strong; being close to bus/metro access and main pedestrian routes further increases potential volume.

**Why 0.39:**
- This is near the top of the recommended 0.35–0.40 range for coffee shops, reflecting that this specific location type (high-profile lifestyle street + curated complex) is **especially traffic-sensitive**.
- We stop short of 0.40–0.45, which would be more typical for **pure convenience or QSR** formats where almost everything hinges on traffic and speed. Here, ambiance and brand still matter, so we leave some weight for customer and competition.

### 3. Competition (0.30)

**Role for this concept:** Important but secondary to traffic and customer fit.

- Wukang Road and surrounding Xuhui areas are known for **dense F&B and café offerings**. There will likely be multiple coffee and tea options within walking distance.
- However, this area also functions as a **destination cluster**: multiple cafés can coexist and even reinforce each other by making the area more attractive overall.
- Differentiation is possible through:
  - Product quality (coffee, pastries, light meals)
  - Ambiance and interior design
  - Service style and brand story
  - Niche offerings (e.g., specialty beans, desserts, or co-working friendly environment)
- Because the concept is not described as a low-price, commodity coffee outlet, **price-based competition** is less central than experience and brand.

**Why 0.30:**
- This is the **low end of the coffee-shop competition range (0.30–0.35)**, reflecting that while competition is present, the area’s destination nature and potential for differentiation reduce the relative weight compared to traffic.
- A higher weight (0.33–0.35) would be more appropriate in a highly saturated, non-destination urban strip where cafés are largely interchangeable and price/promotion wars dominate.
