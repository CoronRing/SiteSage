- **Strong office worker base:**
  - **30,000–40,000 office workers** within 1 km; **~8,000** directly in White Magnolia tower.

- **Integrated into a major mixed-use landmark:**
  - White Magnolia Plaza is a key **North Bund landmark**, providing stable long-term demand rather than relying only on tourists.
- **Ground-floor position:**
  - L1 placement close to main flows reduces friction for grab-and-go purchases.
- **Residential buffer:**
  - **~16k residents within 500 m** and **~59k within 1 km** create a long-term neighborhood customer base, supporting mornings plus evenings and weekends.
- **Tourist & cruise spillover:**
  - While not core, cruise passengers and skyline tourists add **incremental, higher-spending** coffee demand.

### 7.2 Cons / Risks

- **High competition within mall and nearby F&B:**
  - Presence of **multiple F&B outlets, convenience stores (7‑11), and likely other coffee chains** increases customer fragmentation.  
  - Achieving **>4% conversion** may require strong differentiation (quality, speed, brand).
- **Office market volatility:**
  - North Bund Grade A offices show **15–25% vacancy** in recent reports; economic downturn or slower leasing reduces office worker base.  
  - If occupancy lags, total potential may stay closer to the **deterministic lower band**.
- **Mall footfall distribution:**
  - Actual **internal routing** (whether main flows pass the shop) is crucial. A store slightly off the main commuter route could see effective footfall below its theoretical catchment.  
- **Cruise passenger seasonality:**
  - Cruise-related traffic is **seasonal and episodic**, not reliable for everyday projections.
- **Elderly-heavy local demographics:**
  - Around **20–21% aged 65+** — this segment may have **lower frequency** of boutique coffee consumption, especially in morning peak, limiting residential upside.
