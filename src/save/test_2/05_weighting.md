## Domain-by-Domain Rationale

### 1. Customer (0.31)

**Rubric reference:** Coffee shops typically allocate ~0.30–0.33 to customer.

**Contextual reasoning:**
- The shop is in a **large mall in Minhang**, so the customer base will be a mix of:
  - Mall shoppers (families, young adults, couples)
  - Office workers in surrounding buildings
  - Local residents using the mall as a leisure and shopping destination
- The concept, as described, does **not** indicate a highly niche or luxury positioning; it likely offers a **broad-appeal coffee menu** at mainstream or mid-range pricing.
- Because of this broad appeal:
  - Precise demographic targeting (e.g., only high-income or only students) is **less critical** than it would be for a boutique or premium specialty café.
  - However, overall **density of potential customers**, their spending power, and their propensity for café usage still matter significantly (e.g., concentration of young professionals, white-collar offices, students nearby).

Thus, customer is assigned **0.31**: important but not dominant, reflecting that the shop needs a sizable and appropriate audience, but can serve a fairly wide demographic range typical for mall coffee venues.

### 2. Traffic & Accessibility (0.39)

**Rubric reference:** Coffee shops: 0.35–0.40, with higher values for concepts reliant on foot traffic and commuting.

**Contextual reasoning:**
- Being located in **Zhongsheng World Mall**, daily **foot traffic is the primary demand driver**:
  - Shoppers passing by the store on Level 1
  - People using the mall as a corridor between transit points and nearby offices or residences
  - Visitors taking breaks from shopping or meetings
- Coffee purchases tend to be **habitual and opportunistic**:
  - Grab-and-go orders while entering/leaving the mall
  - Quick stops between errands
  - Casual meetups and short breaks
- Within a mall, **micro-location** is crucial:
  - Visibility from main entrances, escalators, and key corridors
  - Proximity to anchor tenants or high-traffic zones
  - Ease of wayfinding and line-of-sight from common areas
- While street parking and roadside visibility are less relevant in a fully indoor mall, **internal accessibility and natural flow of people** are just as important.

Because of this strong reliance on incidental and habitual traffic, **Traffic & Accessibility** receives the highest weight of **0.39**, near the upper end of the coffee-shop guideline range.

### 3. Competition (0.30)

**Rubric reference:** Coffee shops: typically 0.30–0.35, higher in saturated urban markets.

**Contextual reasoning:**
- In a Shanghai mall environment, it is **likely** there will be:
  - Other beverage and dessert outlets (bubble tea, juice bars, bakeries)
  - Possibly another coffee brand (domestic or international)
- The coffee category is generally competitive, but in a strong mall location:
  - **High total footfall** can support multiple players if demand is robust.
  - The shop can differentiate on **quality, ambiance, service, menu variety, or pricing**.
- Unlike a pure roadside strip of nearly identical outlets, a mall's **tenant mix** often includes varied formats and concepts, which can reduce direct one-to-one competition.
- The absence of explicit description of the brand (e.g., undifferentiated low-price chain vs. premium third-wave) suggests a **balanced view**: competition is important, but not the single overriding determinant.

Therefore, competition is set at **0.30**, the lower edge of the "moderate" range. This acknowledges:
- Competition is meaningful in an urban, mall-based coffee context.
- But if **traffic and customer fit** are strong, a competent operator can still succeed even with several nearby alternatives.
