## Market Saturation Assessment

### 1. Does this business benefit from agglomeration (more similar stores)?

Coffee is a **“cluster‑friendly”** category in urban China:

- Multiple coffee & tea brands in a single mall:  
  - In Raffles City North Bund, B2 and upper levels together host **at least 6 distinct coffee brands** plus 3–4 dessert/bakery competitors.  
  - This concentration turns the mall into a recognized **“beverage cluster”**, which raises overall consumer visitation for drinks and snacks.
- For your concept, this means:
  - **Brand‑driven / differentiated cafés** (e.g., specialty single‑origin, themed interiors, co‑working angle) often benefit from neighbors like Manner, Starbucks, and Luckin because:
    - Customers already come to the mall with “I’ll have a coffee” in mind.  
    - If you can stand out on **experience or niche offering**, you capture a slice of that pre‑existing demand.

Therefore:

- For a **standard mass coffee counter** with similar pricing/format to Manner or Luckin: **additional similar stores are more harmful than helpful** – the category is already oversupplied on B2.  
- For a **differentiated concept** (e.g., slow‑bar specialty, third‑place coworking, brunch‑oriented café), **co‑location is positive**, as the cluster increases mall‑wide draw.

### 2. Can the customer base support another competitor?

From previous analysis:

- Potential for your unit:  
  - Mid‑case: **≈475 cups/day**, revenue **14,000–17,000 RMB/day**.  
  - High‑case (strong execution, good visibility): **≈900–1,000 cups/day**, revenue **27,000–35,000 RMB/day**.
- Number of coffee‑forward outlets in 1 km: **≈25–30**.

If each shop averages:

- Conservative **300 cups/day** (accounting for weaker independents) → 30 shops = **9,000 cups/day**.  
- High **500 cups/day** → 30 shops = **15,000 cups/day**.

Given >60,000 reachable people:

- Per‑capita demand required at 12,000 cups/day is:  
  - 12,000 / 60,000 = **0.2 cups/person/day** (1.4 cups/week).  
  - This is **plausible** in a CBD with high office worker density and strong mall footfall.

Conclusion:

- **Numerically**, the catchment can support one more coffee shop **if**:
  - You either capture **at least 3–4%** of the mall coffee demand (≈300–500 cups/day), or  
  - You address a niche time window (e.g., earlier breakfast, late evenings) or niche audience (e.g., laptop workers) underserved by others.
- The area is **close to saturation for generic coffee**, but **still has capacity** for a shop with distinct positioning and strong operational execution.
